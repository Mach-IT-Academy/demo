Lesson 1: The Four Functions of Computing

Core Principle: At a fundamental level, computers perform four essential operations: INPUT, PROCESSING, OUTPUT, and STORAGE.

✅ Concept Check

Question: Which component is responsible for PROCESSING in a computer system?

A) Hard Disk Drive
B) Central Processing Unit (CPU)
C) Random Access Memory (RAM)
D) Graphics Card
Explanation: The CPU (Central Processing Unit) is the "brain" of the computer responsible for executing instructions and performing mathematical and logical operations on data. RAM provides temporary storage, the hard drive provides long-term storage, and the graphics card handles video processing.

An IT manager needs to back up 8,500 GB of company data. They're considering purchasing either a 10 TB external drive or multiple smaller drives.
Will the 10 TB drive have enough capacity for all the data?
No — 8,500 GB exceeds 10 TB
Yes — the 10 TB drive has enough capacity

No — a 10 TB drive can only hold 1,000 GB
Correct Answer: Yes, 8,500 GB equals 8.5 TB Explanation: To convert GB to TB, divide by 1,000: 8,500 GB ÷ 1,000 = 8.5 TB. Since 8.5 TB < 10 TB, the drive has sufficient capacity with 1.5 TB (1,500 GB) to spare. Remember: 1 TB = 1,000 GB in decimal notation (which manufacturers use). The 10 TB drive is the right choice and provides headroom for future growth.
